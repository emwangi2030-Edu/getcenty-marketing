Kenyan SME payroll month-end fails on the same recurring details: an attendance edit after cutoff, a stale phone number, an unapproved advance, an M-Pesa failure left unresolved, a statutory line filed late, or a journal that does not reconcile with the bank movement. None of these failures are interesting individually — together, they create the month-end chaos that delays payroll, attracts statutory penalties, and burns trust with staff and auditors. This checklist exists so the same details get caught the same way every month.

To print: in your browser, open this page and use Cmd-P (Mac) or Ctrl-P (Windows / Linux) → Save as PDF or print to paper. A co-branded PDF version for accountants and HR consultants is available on request via the contact form on the Centy site.

How to use this checklist

  • Adopt the cadence, then adapt the items. The structure (T-5 → T-1 → cutoff → run → reconcile → close) holds for most Kenyan SMEs; specific bullets adjust to your sector and workforce mix.
  • Assign owners by role, not by name. Roles survive turnover; named owners do not. Add the role next to each phase header in your printed copy.
  • Tick boxes in real time, not at retrospective. The checklist is preventative, not forensic. A box ticked late is worse than no box at all.
  • Treat exceptions as artefacts. Any item skipped or escalated gets a one-line note with date and approver — the note becomes audit evidence.
  • Refresh the checklist itself quarterly. Statutory or workflow changes should propagate into the checklist within the next month-end.

Phase 1 — T-5 to T-2: pre-cutoff data hygiene

Owner: Payroll administrator. Backup: Finance lead.

  • ☐ Confirm payroll calendar — cutoff date, approval cutoff, pay date, and statutory remittance dates published to the team in writing.
  • ☐ Update employee register — new hires entered with full statutory IDs, leavers terminated with final-pay calculation reviewed, role and rate changes effective-dated.
  • ☐ Validate phone numbers — M-Pesa numbers for every active worker checked against the live register; flagged numbers (failed in last cycle, recently changed) re-verified out-of-band with the worker.
  • ☐ Apply benefits and policy changes — pension uplifts, medical schemes, salary advances, loan deductions, allowances all reflected in the system before cutoff.
  • ☐ Refresh statutory tables — confirm payroll engine has current PAYE bands, Housing levy rate, NSSF tiers, SHIF rate, and any sector-specific obligations applied.
  • ☐ Reconcile time and attendance — last cycle's anomalies (overtime spikes, missed clock-ins, supervisor disputes) closed and documented.
  • ☐ Communicate cutoff — reminder to HR, supervisors, and approvers about the cutoff time and the consequences of late submissions.

Phase 2 — T-1 to T: attendance lock and cutoff

Owner: HR lead + Payroll administrator.

  • ☐ Lock attendance at the published cutoff time. Edits after lock require a documented exception with named approver.
  • ☐ Site / shift supervisor sign-off on attendance for each location, captured in writing or in-system.
  • ☐ Casual-worker hours reviewed — daily and weekly totals match supervisor sign-off; piece-rate counts cross-checked against output reports where applicable.
  • ☐ Overtime, allowances, and bonuses reviewed for the period; only approved items enter the run.
  • ☐ Advances and deductions register reconciled — outstanding advances offset against the current pay; new advances captured.
  • ☐ Statutory edge cases reviewed — leavers, joiners mid-period, returning leave, suspensions, and casual-to-permanent transitions.
  • ☐ Final headcount and gross totals agreed with HR before the run starts.

Phase 3 — T+1 to T+2: gross-to-net calculation and review

Owner: Payroll administrator.

  • ☐ Run gross-to-net in the payroll engine; capture the run reference and date-time.
  • ☐ Statutory deductions calculated: PAYE, Housing levy, NSSF, SHIF, NITA, and any sector-specific deductions applicable.
  • ☐ Net pay totals reconciled against the prior cycle for material variances; differences > the agreed tolerance investigated and explained.
  • ☐ Per-employee payslip review spot-check: random sample of 5–10% of payslips reviewed line by line for accuracy.
  • ☐ Casual-pay batch totals reviewed separately from salaried batch totals.
  • ☐ Edge cases re-reviewed — leavers' final pay, statutory pro-rations for mid-period joiners, advances offset.
  • ☐ Run pack prepared for approver: summary report, statutory totals, exception list, and any policy deviations flagged.

Phase 4 — Approval (maker–checker)

Owner: Finance lead (preparer) → CFO / Founder (approver). Where the team is small, the approver role must be accepted explicitly, not delegated back informally.

  • ☐ Preparer signs off on the run pack — totals, statutory split, exceptions, deviations.
  • ☐ Approver reviews independently — not as a rubber stamp; spot-checks at least one exception case in detail.
  • ☐ Variance vs prior period explained in writing (one paragraph per material driver).
  • ☐ Statutory remittance schedule confirmed — what is owed to which agency by what date.
  • ☐ Disbursement batch composition reviewed — which employees on M-Pesa, which on bank transfer, fallback rails noted.
  • ☐ Approval recorded in the payroll system or a signed register, with date-time and approver identity.

Phase 5 — Disbursement execution (M-Pesa + bank)

Owner: Finance lead (preparer) + Approver (releaser). See bulk M-Pesa payroll disbursements Kenya for the operational pattern.

  • ☐ Pre-execution checks — float / bank balance covers the batch; idempotency key set; no duplicate batch reference.
  • ☐ Submit M-Pesa batch on the rail in operation under contract; capture provider reference.
  • ☐ Submit bank transfer batch (for employees paid by bank) — same maker–checker discipline.
  • ☐ Per-line response captured — success / failure / pending recorded against each line, with timestamps.
  • ☐ Failure triage — each failure investigated within the cycle: confirm number with supervisor or HR, attempt retry once, decide on alternate rail or next-cycle defer.
  • ☐ Payslip distribution — payslips sent (email or print) on the schedule communicated to staff.
  • ☐ Communications — finance posts the payday-complete confirmation to the relevant internal channel.

Phase 6 — Reconciliation (three-way match)

Owner: Finance lead.

  • ☐ Payroll register total = sum of expected net lines for the period (excluding deferrals).
  • ☐ M-Pesa statement total for the run matches register, within agreed tolerance for fees and timing.
  • ☐ Bank statement reflects the funding movement that supported M-Pesa float + direct bank transfers.
  • ☐ Differences explained in writing per batch ID: retries, reversals, fee allocation, timing.
  • ☐ Exception queue updated — every open failure has an owner, an SLA, and a dated note.
  • ☐ Fee accounting applied per policy (separate expense line or netted against wages — consistent with prior periods).

Phase 7 — Statutory remittance preparation

Owner: Payroll administrator + statutory reviewer. This phase is parallel to disbursement, not a follow-on.

  • ☐ PAYE — return prepared with current bands; filed via iTax by the statutory deadline.
  • ☐ Housing levy — calculated against the relevant base and filed by the statutory deadline.
  • ☐ NSSF — employer + employee contributions remitted under current tier rules.
  • ☐ SHIF — contributions remitted under current rules.
  • ☐ NITA — levy paid where applicable.
  • ☐ Sector-specific obligations — any additional levies relevant to the business (e.g. industry training, regulatory) calculated and remitted.
  • ☐ Withholding tax on payments to applicable categories of service provider — separate from payroll but reviewed in the same window for completeness.
  • ☐ Filing receipts retained in the document store with the period folder.
  • ☐ Statutory reconciliation — what was withheld in payroll matches what was filed and paid; differences documented.

Phase 8 — Accounting close

Owner: Finance lead.

  • ☐ Journal entries posted — payroll, statutory liabilities, M-Pesa fees, advances offset, accruals.
  • ☐ Batch IDs in journal narrations — each posting line references the originating batch.
  • ☐ Trial balance check — payroll liabilities and clearing accounts net to expected values; outstanding items explained.
  • ☐ Accounting system reconciled to payroll register for the period.
  • ☐ Expense management overlap — site allowances and supervisor floats reconciled where they intersect with payroll (see expense management Kenya SMEs).
  • ☐ Period locked in the accounting system; reopening requires documented exception.

Phase 9 — Audit retention and learning

Owner: Finance lead. Lightweight but non-skippable.

  • ☐ Period folder archived — payroll reports, statutory returns + receipts, M-Pesa exports, bank statements, exceptions log, approval evidence.
  • ☐ Audit trail check — every material decision traceable to a named owner with a timestamp.
  • ☐ One-page retrospective — what slipped this month, what to change before next cutoff.
  • ☐ Action items logged for the next cycle (data hygiene, policy clarification, training).
  • ☐ Refresh the checklist itself if a statutory or workflow change should land before next month-end.

Common failure modes (worth printing on the back of the page)

  • Late attendance edits — fix: enforce hard cutoff with named exception approver.
  • Stale M-Pesa numbers — fix: re-verify on every change, reject batch lines with same-day number edits.
  • Unapproved advances — fix: register-only deductions; no parallel sheet.
  • Statutory paid after disbursement-day rush — fix: parallel-track owner from cutoff.
  • Reconciliation done at month-three retrospectively — fix: same-cycle three-way match before close.
  • No record of who approved what — fix: approver identity captured in-system or signed register, every time.
  • Failed M-Pesa lines left open for next cycle without decision — fix: failure-triage SLA inside the cycle.

What to take away

A clean payroll month-end in a Kenyan SME is not the result of heroics on the last day — it is the cumulative effect of a checklist run every month with the same discipline, locking data at cutoff, enforcing maker–checker, treating disbursement and statutory as parallel tracks, reconciling each cycle before opening the next, and retaining evidence as a default. Print this page, paste it on the wall, tick the boxes, and the worst month-end gets dull instead of dramatic.
